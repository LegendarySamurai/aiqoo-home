Our team is here to assist you by answering the most frequently asked questions.
Social distancing measures are recommended during the pandemic; however, home repair and renovation projects should not be neglected. Following the guidelines provided by the federal and state advisors will greatly assist you in keeping safe:

Maintain the 6-foot separation recommendation during their visit. Ask that the service provider and their team wear protective masks (and possibly gloves) during their full stay in your home and for your safety, consider wearing a mask yourself as well.

Finally, sanitize and disinfect all work areas and frequently touched fixtures (doorknobs, light switches etc.) after the visit.

Relevant service providers operating in your area received your contact information automatically once you have completed all questions.

Different contractors have diverse call-back return policies and individual work hours. Though we cannot commit to a time-frame on their behalf, our experience proves most providers attempt to call within the first 3 hours of receiving contact information or at the beginning of their next work day if contact information is left outside office hours.

We strongly suggest you make yourself available to receive calls to assure prompt response to your request.

Financing is obviously dependent on the type and size of project in question.

There is a host of financing options available today for home renovation projects dependent on differing project and individual borrower criteria.

The great news is that most esteemed service providers are also serving today as expert financing advisors and have the knowledge and tools to provide you not only with the best advice on the different available options but also walk you through the full process to the successful completion of the loan request.

There is no doubt that making a wise and informed choice eases the path to a successful and positive project completion.

Whatever the size of your project, we always recommend the following:

  • - Speak with each service provider yourself and check his credentials, certifications and state licenses to ascertain that he is able to complete the project at hand.

  • - Its best practice to get several quotes (an average of 3 quotes for a medium to large project is best), compare costs and understand the full scope of the project before making your informed decision.

  • - Good communication is the backbone for a great project. Select a provider you feel comfortable with. Remember the service provider you choose will be entering your home, so make the wise choice, accordingly.

  • - Also, check out the recommended list of questions to ask about the project below


Do not hesitate to ask the service contractor questions that will assist you in making a choice. The list below is a good way to start:

  • - How many similar projects have you done in the past?

  • - Is a permit required for the project?

  • - What is the realistic project timeline?

  • - What can I expect in terms of hours of work per day?

  • - How many workers will there be?

  • - Is there any kind of warrantee for materials and products?

  • - Is there a warrantee for the actual project?

  • - What is the payment schedule?
